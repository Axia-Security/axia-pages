1. Brand Overview

Core positioning, pillars, and brand architecture

Core Positioning Statement

AXIA is the AI-powered judgment layer for insider risk. It connects to your existing tools, understands what DLP can't — intent, context, and behavior — and turns data into decisions. Where DLP generates alerts, AXIA delivers verdicts. Where tools block or ignore, AXIA engages, coaches, and escalates.

"The Judgment Layer for Insider Risk."

The Four Pillars

👑

CONTEXT

"Context is King"

WHO sent it. WHAT it contains. WHO received it. That's how you find the right cases and assign the right severity.

💬

ENGAGES

"not just alerts"

AXIA doesn't just notify your SOC. It can coach employees, alert managers, and escalate intelligently.

✅

RESOLVES

"cases, not queues"

Every case moves toward resolution. Not just triaged. Not just acknowledged. Actually resolved.

🛡️

GUIDES

"Guides Security Systems"

AXIA guides your security systems via Smart Blocks — context-aware policies that improve over time.

Name Etymology

AXIA derives from Greek axios (worthy/valuable) — the root of axiology (the study of value and judgment). AXIA determines what's worth investigating. When employees trigger a DLP alert, AXIA judges whether it's worthy of attention, worthy of escalation, or worthy of action.

2. Brand Principles

The foundation for all AXIA visual and verbal identity

1. Vigilant, Not Anxious

Our visuals convey calm alertness. We're always watching, but never panicking. Dark, focused interfaces with purposeful motion.

2. Judgment Over Enforcement

We don't just detect — we decide. Our brand reflects intelligence and discernment, not just protection.

3. Human-Centered AI

Technology that coaches rather than blocks. Our visual language is warm enough to feel approachable, professional enough to trust.

4. Clarity in Complexity

Security is complex. Our design makes it comprehensible. Clean hierarchy, clear signals, actionable insights.

5. Trusted Advisor Presence

We speak like a trusted advisor in the room — confident but not arrogant, direct but not aggressive.

3. Logo

The Scanning Lens mark: concentric rings for layered analysis, crosshairs for precision without aggression

Primary Logo: Scanning Lens

AXIA

Scanning Lens + Wordmark

Design Rationale

  • Concentric rings = layered analysis. AXIA examines data at multiple depths.
  • Crosshairs = precision without aggression. Focused, not threatening.
  • Central dot = the core insight. The verdict at the center of analysis.
  • Lens metaphor = insight, not enforcement. AXIA sees and understands.
  • Aligned with "Intelligent Watchguard" positioning direction.

"We noticed unusual activity. Let's review together."

Warm, non-accusatory, collaborative.

Words to Use

CategoryPreferred Terms
CapabilityJudgment, decision, context, intent, engagement, orchestration
ValueClarity, insight, intelligence, understanding, guidance
RelationshipPartner, advisor, copilot, assistant
DifferentiationHuman-in-loop, context-aware, behavioral
OutcomeResolution, coaching, escalation, protection

Words to Avoid

CategoryAvoidWhy
Aggressive"Catch," "Hunt," "Eliminate"Too adversarial; conflicts with human-centric positioning
Surveillance"Monitor," "Track," "Watch" (as primary verbs)Privacy concerns; surveillance connotations
Generic AI"Revolutionary AI," "State-of-the-art ML"Overused; doesn't differentiate
Fear-based"Threat landscape," "Unprecedented risk"FUD marketing; not our voice
Technical jargon"UEBA," "DSPM," "CASB" (without explanation)Alienates non-technical stakeholders

Do's and Don'ts

DO

  • Speak as a confident advisor
  • Lead with outcomes and value
  • Use clear, decisive language
  • Position as partner to security teams
  • Maintain calm authority
  • Be specific with claims and evidence

DON'T

  • Sound like an aggressive vendor
  • Lead with features and technology
  • Use jargon without explanation
  • Position as replacement for teams
  • Use fear, uncertainty, doubt (FUD)
  • Be vague or make unsubstantiated claims

11. Competitive Visual Positioning

"Beyond the Generic Brain" framework

ABANDON

  • Brain/Neural — Overused, suggests passive intelligence
  • Shield — Suggests defense, not judgment
  • Lock — Suggests blocking, not engagement
  • Generic AI — Doesn't differentiate from competitors

EXPLORE

  • Lens/Eye — Insight, understanding, clarity
  • Compass — Guiding decisions, direction
  • Convergence — Lines meeting at decision point
  • Abstract "A" — Ownable, unique to AXIA
  • Connection Nodes — Orchestration, partnership

Market vs. AXIA

DimensionCurrent MarketAXIA Opportunity
ColorBlue-dominant (6 of 8 competitors)Deep Teal + Cyan differentiation
SymbolismShields, locks, generic brainsLens, hub, insight-focused forms
PositioningDetection & blockingJudgment & engagement
ToneVendor-speak, feature-focusedTrusted advisor, outcome-focused
UI ModeLight-first (enterprise default)Dark-first (SOC reality)
Human ElementTechnology-centricHuman-centered AI

Why Teal Differentiates

  • Distinct from blue-dominant competitors (MIND, Cyera, Netskope, Proofpoint)
  • Teal signals "technology + trust" without cold blue
  • Cyan provides energy and modern feel
  • Deep teal base works beautifully in dark mode
  • Optional orange adds human warmth when needed
  • Full palette already validated in product UI

Appendix B: Brand Direction Archive

Four strategic directions were explored. "Intelligent Watchguard" was selected.

CHOSEN

Intelligent Watchguard

Teal + Cyan

AI-powered vigilance, smart protection

Human-Centered AI

Employee coaching, behavioral insight

Decision Command

Operational command, real-time control

Why Intelligent Watchguard Was Chosen

  • Already validated in product UI — The product already uses this palette and dark-first approach.
  • Distinct from competitors — Teal + Cyan is unique in a blue-dominated market.
  • Dark mode native — Works beautifully in SOC environments and matches how users actually experience the product.
  • Modern without being trendy — Will age well while still feeling current.
  • Supports the brand principles — "Vigilant, not anxious" is perfectly expressed through calm teal tones.
  • Scalable — Works across product, marketing, sales, and corporate materials.
