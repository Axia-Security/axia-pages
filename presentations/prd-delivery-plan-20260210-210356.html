Plan: Delivering AXIA PRD into Engineering Systems

Context

AXIA has a 4-person engineering team that's shipped 260+ stories in 3 months and is entering deployment prep. Their JIRA (project MVP, 346+ issues, 23 epics) is organized by engineering domain (Ingestion Service, Analysis Worker, Frontend Foundation, etc.) with pure implementation stories ("Implement X", "Add Y support"). Their Confluence has 38 pages - mostly dashboard specs that Aliaksei builds from.

Meanwhile, we have PRD v2.0 (AXIA/Product/AXIA-PRD-V7.md) - a comprehensive product specification with 22 sections, 19 user stories with acceptance criteria, organized by product screens (Command, Case Details, Insights, Intel, Enforce, Posture, Connect, Settings) plus a new Release Phases & Scope section (Section 4) defining P1/P2/P3 delivery phases, AI/ML engine specs, engagement system, NFRs, integrations, and success criteria.

The problem: Two completely different mental models. Engineering thinks in services and components. Product thinks in user outcomes and screens. There's currently zero product layer in their Atlassian.

The goal: Introduce the product layer without disrupting a team that's shipping fast.

Decisions Made

  • Priority: Pre-label all stories with P1/P2/P3 based on the PRD phase scoping model (P1 = single-interaction AI with manual approval, P2 = user behavioral analysis with automated engagement, P3 = cross-org intelligence with orchestration)
  • Confluence: Full native conversion (not Drive links)
  • Issue type: Stories (establish the right practice)

Phase Definitions (from PRD Section 4)

What We DON'T Do

  • Don't modify existing engineering epics or stories
  • Don't reorganize the existing backlog
  • Don't assign owners (team's decision)
  • Don't create duplicate stories for work already tracked as engineering tasks
  • Don't add process overhead - this is a reference layer
